The Cramp-Free Guide

Written by women, for women. What actually helps.

Do This During Your Period

  • Move gently: Walking or yoga boosts blood flow and cuts prostaglandins.
  • Heat therapy: 20 mins on your lower belly. As effective as ibuprofen for many.
  • Magnesium-rich foods: Dark chocolate, almonds, spinach relax the uterus.
  • Omega-3s: Salmon, chia seeds, walnuts fight inflammation.
  • Stay hydrated: Dehydration makes cramps worse. Aim for 2L water.
  • Ginger tea: 1g of ginger powder = 400mg ibuprofen in studies.

Avoid These Cramp Triggers

  • Excess caffeine: 1 cup OK. 3+ cups constrict blood vessels.
  • Salty snacks: Chips + bloating = more pressure on cramps.
  • Alcohol: Dehydrates and worsens mood swings.
  • Skipping meals: Low blood sugar = more pain sensitivity.
  • Tight waistbands: Wear high-waist, loose pants. Your uterus needs space.

When to See a Doctor

This app helps with normal period pain. Call your doctor if: pain stops your life for 2+ days, bleeding soaks a pad in 1 hour, or you have fever/chills. You know your body best.
